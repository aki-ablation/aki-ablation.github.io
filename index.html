Research

研究内容

Microwave Ablation Zone Imaging (Master's Thesis)

Conventional MWA monitoring methods — ultrasound, CT, MRI — have significant limitations in real-time feedback. I developed a microwave tomography approach that uses the time difference of arrival (TDOA) of signals before and after ablation to estimate the treated region, then dramatically reduces the number of unknowns in the tomographic reconstruction. This enabled accurate imaging of the electrical properties of ablated tissue.

Patient-Specific Pre-Treatment Planning (Ph.D., GWU)

At GWU, my work focuses on numerical simulation-based pre-treatment planning for liver cancer MWA. By incorporating individual anatomical data and tissue properties into COMSOL simulations, the goal is to optimize microwave power and duration for each patient — enabling safer, more reliable treatments. This is complemented by hands-on MWA antenna fabrication and ex vivo tissue experiments. Looking ahead, I aim to collaborate with clinical partners toward eventual clinical trials.
